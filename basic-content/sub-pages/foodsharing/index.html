Facts

In Denmark alone, 700,000 tons of food is wasted each year that could have been eaten. The food waste costs Danish consumers more than 11 billion DKK a year. The households waste more than 260,000 tons of food per year and the food industry 133,000 tons. That is why food waste is a huge problem, which is not only expensive, but also exhausting of our resources and our environment.

One of the biggest reasons people throw away food is they don't know the difference between "sell before"(best before), "best by", and "use by" dates. The "use by" date is the one to pay attention to! This date tells you when the food is no longer safe to consume. However, the "sell before" and "best by" dates are actually best guesses as to food quality—not food safety. It is likely perfectly safe to eat for days or weeks after that "sell-by" or "best-by" date has passed. So even if your eggs say they are "best by" a certain date, they don’t suddenly turn rotten at midnight.

Click on the cards below to see facts about different types of foods

Bread

Shelf Life:

Bakery bread: 2-3 days past bbd (Best Before Date)
Plain bread: 5-7 days past bbd

Storage:

Room temperature (not in the fridge!)

Freeze:

Airtight container

Is it okay?

Mold? - Not safe to eat
Stale? - Dip in a bowl of water, or run under a sink until the bread is all wet (not soaked) and put in the oven for 5-8 min at around 210°C

Milk

Shelf Life:

4-7 days past best before date

Storage:

Fridge shelf under 4.5°C

Freeze:

Airtight container (remember liquid expands when frozen) Thaw in fridge Use for cooking or baking

Is it okay?

Mold? - Not safe to eat :(
Stale? - Not safe to drink but good for cooking and baking. But use your judgement, if you think it's too bad don't use it.

Cheese

Shelf Life:

Hard cheese: 4–6 m in a fridge.
Medium hard cheese: 2–4 w after expiration date.
Soft cheese: They will have “use by date”.

Storage:

Fridge shelf

Freeze:

Grated cheese is perfect to freeze.

Is it okay?

Mold on soft cheese? - Whole thing is bad.
Mold on hard cheese? - You can simply cut off the mold.

Eggs

Shelf Life:

Up to 5 weeks past best before date

Storage:

Fridge shelf in original packaging. Pointy side facing down to help the yolk stay in the middle.

Freeze:

Beat the eggs up before freezing. Freeze eggs in a muffins tray to keep separate. Egg whites by themselves do well in the freezer but egg yolkes by themselves need to be mixed with sugar or salt before frozen.

Is it okay?

Test the eggs in water before opening. If the eggs sink they're good. If the eggs float they should be thrown out.

Fish

Shelf Life:

Check the packaging

Storage:

Fridge shelf under 4.5°C In original packaging or wrapped tightly

Freeze:

Freeze fish in the original packaging or wrapped tightly.

Is it okay?

Look at the date on the packaging. Signs that the fish has become bad: Slimy, discoloured, bad smell.

Meat

Shelf Life:

Check the packaging

Storage:

Store raw meat and poultry in clean, sealed containers on the bottom shelf of the fridge

Freeze:

Freeze it any time before its use by date defrost in the fridge overnight

Is it okay?

If the meat smells bad, has a greenish hue or is sticky it is most likely not save to eat.

Leafy greens

Shelf Life:

3-5 days past best before date

Storage:

Store in a fridge. To prolong it's life put a paper towel inside the bag. Even better option is to put in an airtight container with a paper towel layered on the top and bottom.

Freeze:

Even though leafy greens don’t hold their texture when frozen it can still be a good idea to freeze fresh herbs or spinach for smoothies or for cooking.

Is it okay?

If the greens become soggy and smell bad they should be removed from the rest and thrown out.

Vegetables

Shelf Life:

Store in the fridge. Except tomatoes and potatoes.

Storage:

Most vegetable benefit from blanching before freezing.

Freeze:

Even though leafy greens don’t hold their texture when frozen it can still be a good idea to freeze fresh herbs or spinach for smoothies or for cooking.

Is it okay?

Mold? - Firm vegetables: simply cut of the molded part
Soft vegetables: not safe to eat :(

Fruits

Shelf Life:

Depends on the type

Storage:

Most fruits keep well in the fridge. But some fruits should ripen on the counter: apricots, avocados, guava, kiwi, mangoes, melons, nectarines, papaya, peaches, bananas and plums

Freeze:

Think about how you will most likely use the fruit (in a dessert, smoothie etc) and prepare it accordingly.

Is it okay?

Mold? - Not safe to eat :(
If the fruit has a bad smell and texture it should be thrown out unless you can cut off the bad part.

Leftovers

Keep your leftovers in an airthight container. Decide when you plan on eating it, if you don't think you will eat it over the next few days consider freezing them
