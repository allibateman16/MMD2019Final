Design

COLOR SCHEME

The color scheme that I decided on was not what I was expecting. In my original Project Work Documentation I expressed that I did not think that I would want the bright colors that are associated with Swiss Style Design because I thought that they were too "energetic" and "bold". Below are the colors that I originally chose for my color scheme.

After working on my website, I started to realize that the soft colors were getting lost in the bright and bold colors of the images. I made the decision to embrace the bold colors and make use of their attention grabbing brightness for headers.

I also decided that I would make more use of the color black in parts of the design other than text. I created borders around images and boxes so that they stand out from the background. I decided to also make use of white in some of my headers. White space is very important in Swiss Style Design and I wanted to make sure that it was a part of mine as well. This was also a part of my decision to do a fairly simple background, keep the white space there to make other elements stand out.

Logos

My logo ideas are simple, minimalist, and brightly colored. I plan on creating something eye-catching, but not too intricate.

TYPOGRAPHY

For the different fonts for my site, I stuck with Helvetica. Since the entirety of Swiss Style Design uses it, I thought that it would be appropriate. I also thought that the fonts Roboto and Raleway had a similar look. I think that Raleway is a bit more "intricate" then Helvetica or Roboto, but it still is clear and concise. All three are very simple, easy to read, but still have a strong presence.

I have decided to use Roboto for my Header1's, Raleway for my Header2's, and Helvetica for my body copy. I have given the option of sans-serif for if my fonts are not working on a certain computer because sans-serif is what everyone was using on their Swiss Design posters and media. The website would still have a similar feeling and look if someone's browser couldn't load the fonts.

Folder Structure

My folder structure is pretty striaght forward. I like to have as few folders as possible, with well-named files within those folders. It just means less clicking around for me. I know that some people like to break it down even further and have folders within folders to really keep things organized, but I feel (for now at least) that I am able to keep everything organized in this fashion.
