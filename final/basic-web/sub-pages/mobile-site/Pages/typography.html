Typography

Konstruktive Grafik was designed mainly for exhibition of Swiss design style. Konstruktive Grafik was designed at a time when Swiss artists wanted to design works of art that would define Swiss’ new design style. Like the USA baut, Konstruktive Grafik was displayed at the Kunstgewerbe Museum in Zurich. Initially, before the introduction of the new style, many external readers of Swiss work could not make sense of the Swiss style and the ideas that it presented. The new design style was therefore an attempt to communicate the ideas of Swiss design to the world.

From ground-breaking magazine Neue Grafik to posters for the concert series Musica Viva at Tonhalle Zurich, Josef Muller-Brockmann’s balanced grids and mathematical ratios created the International Style.

Adrian Frutiger applied specific mathematics to his design concerning the leading, kerning, line height, and overall font structure. He was responsible for creating and perfecting the font face Univers.
